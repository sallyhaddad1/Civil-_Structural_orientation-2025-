Specifying Design Conditions
Design conditions define the upper and lower limits of key parameters - such as temperature and pressure - that equipment and piping must safely and reliably withstand, accounting for normal operation, process fluctuations, and potential upsets.
For example:
1. A vessel operating at 8 barg might be designed for 10 barg.
2. A pipe carrying hot fluid at 120°C might be designed for 150°C.

Process Safety
Process safety focuses on preventing and mitigating catastrophic accidents associated with hazardous industrial processes such as:
- Hydrocarbon/Chemical releases,
- Fires & Explosions,
- or Toxic exposures.

Key Elements of Process Safety
- Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment.
- Engineering Controls and Safeguards.
- Operational Procedures and Training.
